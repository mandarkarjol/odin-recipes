Cabbage Pakoda

Description

Cabbage pakodas are essentially fried cabbage dumplings, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. They are appetizers and pair very well with mint sauce. This recipe is pefect with a hot cup of chai on a rainy Sunday afternoon. It is super easy and only takes 10 minutes of prep time and 5 minutes cook time.

So without further adieu, let's look at what you need to prepare this delicious snack

Ingredients

  • Besan (Chickpeas flour) - 1 cup
  • Cabbage - 500 grams
  • Salt - 1 tsp or to taste
  • Red Chilli Powder - 1 tsp
  • Turmeric - 1 tsp
  • Cilantro - 1/4 cup
  • Onion - 1 small
  • Fresh Lime - 1 small

Steps

  1. Cut cabbage into long thin stips
  2. Transfer the cabbage into a mixing bowl
  3. Add salt, red chilli powder and turmeric to the cabbage and mix thoroughly
  4. Let it sit for a few minutes till the mixture starts to release water
  5. Add besan to the mixture and stir until you have a fairly viscous batter, taste and adjust spices to taste accordingly
  6. In a deep vessel, heat coil on a medium flame
  7. After the oil is hot, make small balls of the batter using your hands and release them slowly into the hot oil
  8. Fry the pakoda balls until they appear caramelized, then remove from the oil to a dish lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil
  9. Once all thec cabbage pakodas are fried, transfer to a serving dish and garnish with cilantro, fresh cut limes and onions
  10. Serve with your favorite mint sauce, and enjoy!
